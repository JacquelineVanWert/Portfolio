Tax Alaska Redesign

Overview

A redesign of the Alaska Tax government site.

Objective

The current Alaska Department of Revenue: Tax Division website is outdated and needed to be brought into the modern age with an overhaul on organization of the website navigation.

Deliverables

The deliverables neccessary included the Proto-Persona, Interview plan & transcripts, Affinity Diagram, Empathy Map, User Persona, User Insight, Problem Statement, Ideation, Feature Priotization Matrix, Value Proposition Canvas, Storyboard, Mind Map, Competitor Analysis, Style Tile, Digital Wireframes, User Tests, Iterations based on User Tests, and a mobile and desktop prototype.

Process

The process used for this project includes the following:

Outcome
Research

We started by identifying our typical user into a proto-persona. After deciding on an Alaskan business owner being our main focus, we were able to determine a typical user path for our proto-persona to focus on (ie. Tax Information). We then completed a heuristic evaluation of the current site to redline annotate current problems with accessibility, design, and any other issues. Our next step was to interview 5 individuals who would be a typical user of this site and organize our findings based on importance to the user and government agency using Figjam.

Definition

Using our research, we were able to identify the main problems in navigation and what our users expect while on the site. With the current design, most users had difficulty finding tax information or would try to search within the site using a non-working search bar. With our issues identified, we completed a heuristic evaluation on the site's current navigation and completed a card sorting activity with users to identify like-groups of content for users to navigate through.

Ideation

Using our card sorting results, we redesigned the new sitemap for the website using Figjam. We then were able to create a new navigation header and footer for the site using our updated sitemap. Creating a moodboard on Invision led us to identify common color palettes used for a government site and helped us determine how to approach our new design. We created a style tile with our ideas of a color palette, gradients, typography, iconography, and button styles and states.

Prototyping

With our new navigation in hand, we could start creating our wireframes. Starting in Figma with a low-fidelity wireframe for each section of the site let us play with the layout until we finialized our ideas into a cohesive design. Updating our wireframes with contents from our style tile and adding images, we were able to form our redesign into its high-fidelity point.

Testing

We had then completed 2 five-second usability tests. Doing this on both our Desktop and Mobile versions, we did discover some things that we could change to make the use of the website more apparent. Creating a hero image displaying our site's objective, and reiterating our design led had addressed the issues shown in the usability tests. Completing three users tests after this fact led us to see increased understanding of the site and navigation.

Outcome

In conclusion, we found that design iterations really come to life after testing and feedback, and make for a better design. Hearing how others critiqued our work led us to be inspired by other designs that made more sense for the site contents. We feel that we have done the Alaska Department of Revenue: Tax Division redesign in a way that accomplished our goals of making it more user friendly to navigate and brought it into the modern age.
